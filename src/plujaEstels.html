Questions

plujaEstels and postaDeSol: What format is the most optimal for this type of image and why? With what compression quality does the image not lose quality? Can we reduce its size even more without losing quality? What would happen if we save the image several times with JPEG format?

JPEG is a common format for photographs and images with gradients, as it supports lossy compression. The optimal compression quality depends on the specific image and the desired balance between file size and image quality. In general, a compression quality setting of 80-90 is often considered a good range. Reducing the size of a JPEG image without losing quality can be challenging, as JPEG is a lossy compression format. Once an image is compressed, some information is lost, and further compression may lead to visible artifacts. Saving an image multiple times in JPEG format can result in cumulative loss of quality, known as generation loss. Therefore, it's best to avoid repeated saving in JPEG if maintaining high image quality is crucial.
