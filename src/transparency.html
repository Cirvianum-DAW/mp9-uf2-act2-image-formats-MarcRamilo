Questions

transparency: What is the most optimal format for this type of image and why? What happens when we save it in JPEG format? If our website has a white background, what format would be the most optimal and why?

PNG is also the optimal format for images with transparency, such as logos or graphics with a transparent background. JPEG does not support transparency and saving an image with transparency as a JPEG results in losing the transparent areas, replacing them with a solid color as in the example. The website has a white background, it would be optimal to keep the image in PNG format with transparency. This ensures that the image blends seamlessly with the white background, maintaining a clean and professional appearance.

Have a look at developerTools' Lighthouse tool. This allows you, among other things, to analyze the loading of the images. Check the performance of the different pages you have created. What does Lighthouse tell about the image related perfomance of your pages? What are the main issues? What are the main recommendations?

  • Lighthouse Image-related Performance Analysis:
    • Performance Score (92): Indicates overall good optimization.
    • Main Metrics:
      • FCP (First Contentful Paint): 2.7s - Quick initial loading, positive user experience.
      • LCP (Largest Contentful Paint): 44.1s - Improved but still relatively high, room for optimization.
      • TBT (Total Blocking Time): 70ms - Low impact on user interactivity.
      • CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift): 0 - Stable layout, no unexpected shifts.
      • SI (Speed Index): 28.3s - Improved but potential for further optimization.
    • Recommendations:
      • Optimize images for file size reduction.
      • Eliminate render-blocking resources for quicker initial loading.
      • Reduce unused CSS to minimize page weight.
      • Properly size images to enhance layout stability.
      • Serve images in next-gen formats for smaller file sizes.
      • Minimize main-thread work for improved overall performance.
    • Additional Observations:
      • Address the prevention of cache restoration for better caching behavior.
      • Minimize third-party usage to reduce main thread blocking.
      • Identify and optimize long main-thread tasks for improved responsiveness.
  • Observations with a Slow 3G Connection:
    • Increased Loading Time for Images: Images may take longer to load, impacting overall page load time.
    • Delayed Rendering of Largest Contentful Image (LCP): The delay in loading the largest image might be more pronounced.
    • Total Blocking Time (TBT) Impact: TBT may increase, affecting user interactivity during image loading.
    • Potential for Additional Layout Shifts: A slow connection might exacerbate layout shift issues, affecting visual stability.
